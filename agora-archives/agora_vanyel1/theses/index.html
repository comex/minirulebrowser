Theses

In December 1994, ChrisM came up with a system by which Nomicians everywhere could be awarded Degrees in Nomic--Associate, Bachelor, Masters, and Doctorate. To do so, one must submit a Thesis; below are listed all submitted Theses.

  • I have now found a copy of Steve's thesis, but I'm not sure if it's the final version that got submitted and voted on... anyway, it's called Breaking the Rules, and it got him his Bachelor's.
  • Pascal's untitled thesis on Agoran economics (Rejected as Associate of Nomic by Proposal 1469, 8 March 1995)
  • Vanyel's Pragmatism and Platonism: Two Approaches to Nomic (Accepted as Bachelor of Nomic by Proposal 1476, 8 March 1995)
  • elJefe's Agora Nomic: Recent History (Late 1994 to Early 1995) (Accepted as Bachelor of Nomic by Proposal 1580, 2 May 1995)
  • Favor's A Completely Formal Nomic (Accepted as Bachelor of Nomic by Proposal 1755, 24 October 1995)
  • Andre's A case of problematic precedence (Submitted 13 Nov 1996)
